Research
Jun – Sep 2025

EV Charging Infrastructure & Consumer Behavior

Worked with a PhD researcher to understand why EV charging supply and demand diverge across California neighborhoods. Engineered an R-based data pipeline converting raw California DMV records into zip-code-level EV adoption metrics categorizing vehicles by manufacturer, fuel type, and plug compatibility to map infrastructure gaps.

Modeled the why: housing density, renter-occupancy rates, income, education, and commute patterns as proxies for adoption behavior. Connected findings to behavioral theory rational inattention, price salience, nudge theory to analyze how price transparency shapes consumer choices and firm pricing strategy.
