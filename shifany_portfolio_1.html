Research Jun – Sep 2025

EV Charging Infrastructure & Consumer Behavior

Worked with a PhD researcher to understand why EV charging supply and demand diverge across California neighborhoods. I engineered an R-based data pipeline converting raw DMV vehicle registration records into zip-code-level EV adoption metrics — categorizing vehicles by manufacturer, fuel type, and plug type to identify infrastructure gaps.

Beyond the data, I modeled the why: housing density, renter-occupancy rates, income, commute patterns, and education as proxies for adoption and peak demand. I then connected findings to behavioral theory — rational inattention, price salience, and nudge theory — to analyze how price transparency shapes consumer charging choices and firm pricing strategy.

Consulting Mar – Jun 2025

Uber Autonomous Ride Adoption Strategy

Led a consulting team analyzing consumer adoption patterns for autonomous ride-hailing across key demographic segments. Combined primary interviews with competitive research to identify adoption barriers and opportunity segments, then delivered a go-to-market strategy projecting 50% rider adoption and 25% new user growth within 6 months of pilot launch.
